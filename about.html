Transforming Healthcare Through Data and Innovation

About Me

Hi, I’m Kevin Nguyen — a driven data scientist, biostatician, and health informatician passionate about improving healthcare through technology and innovation.

I grew up in Orange County, California, and earned my Bachelor of Science in Public Health with a concentration in Epidemiology and a minor in Mathematics from the University of California, San Diego (UCSD). During my undergraduate studies, I strengthened my foundation in data science through my coursework in biostatistics, computational statistics, and data analysis—realizing early on that the future of healthcare would be shaped by data and technology.

Currently, I’m pursuing my Master of Science in Health and Clinical Informatics at Oregon Health & Science University (OHSU) in Portland, Oregon. Health informatics represents the perfect intersection of the fields I am passionate about: healthcare to give back to the community, data to apply analytical insights, and technology to drive the future of medicine.

Throughout my academic and professional career, I've worked on a range of projects, from HIV network modeling to building dashboards for state health authorities and analyzing COVID-19 exposure notification data alongside partners like Google, Apple, UC San Diego Health, and the California Department of Public Health. I am skilled in R, Python, Matlab, JavaScript, Tableau, SPSS, ArcGIS, and R Shiny, and have hands-on experience in machine learning, network modeling, and predictive analytics.

My ultimate goal is to pioneer new ways to integrate healthcare, data, and technology—unlocking innovations that improve health outcomes for all.
